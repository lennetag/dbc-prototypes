Funding pathfinder service

This service is part of a pilot run by DSIT, GDS and HMT.

It’s only for teams that have been invited to take part in the funding pathfinder programme. You must also have departmental agreement to use this as an alternative funding approach.

What the service does

The service provides digital support for:

  • incremental funding for innovation and technology
  • portfolio or programme funding
  • incremental funding for live services

It uses existing business case guidance, including the 12-page Willetts Review format and the Green Book, but is designed to be more flexible. You provide more detail as your project matures.

How it works

You can complete the service over multiple sessions. Draft cases are available from your dashboard.

The questions you’re asked will depend on:

  • the type of request you’re making
  • the delivery stage of your work (for incremental cases)
  • whether you can provide information now or later

The service will ask you about:

  • strategy, problem statement and users
  • options analysis and economic model
  • commercial approach
  • financial analysis and accounting
  • management and delivery approach
  • people, departments and governance
  • compliance and impact assessment
  • transition to live service
  • document upload
  • approvals and comments

For live service funding requests, you’ll also need to describe:

  • the current service and how it performs
  • your goals for the next phase

Before you start

You’ll need to have:

  • confirmation that your team has been invited to take part in the pathfinder
  • agreement from your department to use this funding approach
  • an outline of your project or programme, including its aims and delivery stage
  • any early risk, dependency or constraint information
  • access to your project’s financial data and governance arrangements

You may also find it useful to have:

  • your project or programme plan
  • an existing risk log or delivery roadmap

You do not need to upload documents unless asked. The structured information you provide is usually enough.
